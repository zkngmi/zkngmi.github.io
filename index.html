My Legacy Work

27 years as a consultant in supply chain/ERP software projects working for under-the-radar, backbone-of-America, non-tech native companies. These companies have few, if any CS majors. Nobody wants to claim "tech expertise", lest they get stuck with all the hardest work. There's no understanding or interest in basic software concepts such as beta testing, rather there is a broad expectation of iphone-style, bug-free ease of use. The smartest people have long ago left for more exciting industries. Yet these projects provide the chance to profoundly affect the lives of the lowest-paid workers - to put humanity into the most soul-sucked corners of the corporate abyss.

Example: in every one of my clients, chaos reigns in the supply/distribution warehouse. The robotic automation of Amazon and Tesla are not even a vague dream at ordinary company budgets. Workers live paycheck-to-paycheck as they struggle heroicly to fulfill complex customer orders using antiquated systems and/or paper-based processes. Because of bug-ridden or non-existent software systems, they often have no choice but to work until midnight and/or on the weekends, sacrificing their own lives for corporate profits. By replacing paper with handheld scanners and a super-simple UI (that connects to the ERP and actually works), you allow workers the one thing they request - the ability to do their job. In doing so, you simplify thier lives and allow them to spend time with their families. The appreciation they express is real and is something you don't forget.

My primary role is the ERP version of a Product Manager. I've taken dozens of projects from start to finish: discovery, planning, road mapping, evangelizing, tech specification, development, testing, go-live, and post go-live support. When you own a process, there are new challenges every day. The biggest challenge is always inspiring and leading other humans - the idea of software writing itself is still a distant dream. At the end of the day, it is still human beings that take software work from idea to reality.

I started my career as a Developer for 7 years. Knowledge of underlying tech makes you a highly valuable contributor to a team. I've also worked as a Solution Architect, a Project Manager, and Director of IT. A few clients: Dairy Farmers of America (largest dairy supplier in US), Weatherford International (oilfield services), Columbia Pipe (Chicago's largest pipe supplier, recently acquired). Coca-cola and Dole Foods are a bit more familiar names.
